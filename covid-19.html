COVID-19

NovaCrypt + COVID-19

In the midst of the COVID-19 crisis, we seeked to help those struggling to fight the virus. Medical professionals lacked critical PPE and civilians were unable to receive the proper care and safety they needed to stay healthy. With over 3.7 million confirmed cases, and 250,000 deaths, the world has met one of the deadliest viruses in human history. With infection rates skyrocketing, and hospitals running out of supplies, immediate and swift action was needed.

At that point, some students found it necessary to use their skills to make a change, or help out the medical professionals and people during the crisis. NovaCrypt’s founder, Roland Yan, began his research to aid the Coronavirus outbreak in March, just as the tensions around the U.S. began to rise. From New York, he directly witnessed the effect of the N95 mask shortage for the civilian, but most importantly, medical population. Rushing to ship lab supplies from across the world, he moved along with his mentors at Columbia University, to set up a lab in his garage. After 1.5 months of testing, up to 1580+ pairs of data points graphed and calculated, he concluded that N95 masks could be disinfected through oven disinfection (78 degrees C, 30 min.), for up to ten cycles with no compromise in mask filtration efficiency. He also found that wearing masks normally leaked massively around the edges, and so he developed a nose-clip to protect civilians from the virus. The information was rushed to be published in the International Society of Respiratory Protection, and Columbia University’s Earth Institute Blog (link both).

Afterwards, he began NovaCrypt, focusing on Project MediAir and NYFightingCovid to help fight the virus. NYFightingCovid is a movement based in Rockland/Orange/Putnam County where we work to service the Emergency services in our area. Our goal is to make, print, coordinate, and deliver crucial PPE to the medical professionals fighting at our frontlines!
