Project MediAir


MediAir

In the face of the COVID-19 crisis, we saw an exponentially growing number of infected, and an immense strain on the medical professionals and community. Families, businesses, and events, all went into lockdown, slowing the economy and threatening our lives. Medical professionals faced the problem of ferrying critical supplies in shortage from one hospital to another in time, and civilians counted their finances and braced for debt. Those financially stable also faced the problem of receiving crucial medicine that kept them alive and risked their own and family’s lives with every step outside to get it. Those who needed urgent drugs awaiting a delivery halted as other families stocked up on less important supplies.

Our own competitions and events that we dreamed of competing in were shut down. Dreams of meeting like-minded individuals and the experiences of amazing ceremonies were crushed by the virus’s deadly spread. So we took this time (after crying a bit), to begin this project, where we can use our skills in engineering, programming, algorithms, and design to help more than just our own community.

The MediAir project will combat the shortages of supplies, and the dangerously slow travel times needed for medical professionals to transfer critical equipment and civilians to receive much needed PPE and medical supplies like medicine. We are working to reduce that time immensely, and to be able to service multiple high-emergency areas with nearly instantaneous response times up to 1 hour. We have currently tested 2 fully operational models and will be working to prepare and test a third before having in-service testing in some areas in the US. We plan to publicly release information around 5/25, and fully release our project around 6/7/20!
